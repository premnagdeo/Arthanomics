THE LIGHTHOUSE PROJECT

‘Volunteering is an Attitude, not an Action.’

In The Artha Series Episode 1, Arthanomics hosted a live session with Trishya Screwvala, Founder of The Lighthouse Project- a Mumbai based not-for-profit organisation connecting working professionals and college students with children from under resourced communities, through one-on-one mentoring in a safe environment. In this extremely inspiring session, Trishya talked about the noble cause that is volunteering and it’s absolute importance.

Wanting to come up with a platform that channelled the energy, the intention and the very skills today’s generation has, along with their want to ‘give back’ attitude, and at the same time being feasible for those willing to volunteer, Trishya started The Lighthouse Project in 2013, that has since changed the course of volunteering in India!The plan of action, being a one-on-one mentoring programme, has turned out to be extremely effective, building a positive and nurturing relationship between the mentors and their respective mentees.

According to Trishya, in today’s bustling life, volunteering is like a breath of fresh air as it pushes one to keep aside all their problems and limitations that are forever present, and bring in their A game; their best version possible! The viewers were given a new direction, a renewed perspective on the importance of volunteering and the path they could choose.

Needless to say, it was quite an insightful experience!

INNER PLANET

In The Artha Series Episode 2 and 4, Arthanomics hosted a live session and a couple of webinars with Zohra Master and Yogita Madan, Founders of Inner Planet- a psychotherapy firm focused on helping one improve mental health. Inner Planet, through means of understanding, acceptance encouragement and motivation, helps a person to deal with distress, realise their true potential, and grow!

As we interacted about the importance of Mental Health Awareness, viewers got an in depth understanding of Anxiety Issues as well as Depression-their signs, causes and how to overcome them in a detailed and well explained manner. In a very interactive and interesting session, Zohra and Yogita managed to engage every participant through means like collective activities, a Q and A session and encouraging participation altogether with use of relatable examples and situations that viewers could truly connect with!

Safe to say that we’re one step closer to doing away with the stigma and misunderstandings when it comes to Mental Health Issues, and through positivity and zeal grow together!

HERITAGE FABRICS

In the Artha Series Episode 3, Arthanomics hosted a live session with Amaan Jhaveri, Aditya Somani and Rohan Somani, Founders of The Heritage Fabrics Company-a South Bombay based startup, offering millennials a reimagined and iconic collection of everyday looks. They are young entrepreneurs looking to dent the world of fashion, adhering to their motto- "Think of the present, age is just a number!”

They advocated that it is imperative for one to strike a balance between their academic and entrepreneurial endeavours so that they can be proactive in their business while ensuring their personal growth. When asked how they dealt with competition, they emphasised on the importance of being unique and different in the value one creates, believing that their customisation process, response to feedback and price point helps them stand out! They also greatly advocate eco friendly products and believe in ethical consciousness to be a huge contributing factor to brand building.

Their advice to budding young entrepreneurs is- "Start somewhere! There are many reasons to not do something you love- but be prepared to face them with your grit, tenacity and determination to reach success! Always remember to back yourself, because unless you don't, nobody else will."

NOT YOUR NEWSPAPER

In The Artha Series Episode 5, Arthanomics hosted a webinar with Sarah Modak and Natasha Maheshwari, Founders of Not Your Newspaper-a digital platform that aims to simplify the news and current affairs for the average citizen. With their extremely detailed and informative presentation, they were all set to go!

During the webinar there was a meticulous explanation of what political polarisation means and how, in every aspect of our lives- be it marriage or citizenship to simply our attire- politics, or simply the policies made by political leaders, affects our every move, every decision! Further, viewers learnt how the process of dividing the society into poles works and how very common it is all around the globe. Backed by facts and examples, Sarah and Natasha explained how media- from news channels to social media platforms like twitter- aim to influence and micro target individuals to lean towards a particular direction- an eye opener indeed!

They concluded by discussing ways of how we can help break the chain of misinformation, widen our perspectives and keep ourselves poles away only from one thing- dirty politics!

CAMPUS FRANCE

In The Artha Series Episode 7, Arthanomics hosted a webinar with Vikash Golla, Manager of Campus France, Mumbai. Campus France is a government agency operating under the French Ministry of External Affairs that helps students, looking to study in France, acquire relevant and necessary information about the same through personalised counselling sessions and workshops. They also help students prepare for their visa interviews and help them all along the way! In this time of doubt, that almost all students face while making plans for their future education, Campus France has sought to be a guiding light. From briefing the viewers about France’s geography to a detailed explanation about the ‘grande écoles’, Vikash simplified the broad range of options a student can choose from. Our viewers were highly motivated upon discovering the positive and encouraging relationship the government of India and France share- enabling them to make grand progress in the field of education. From covering topics like steps on how to search for a suitable program and everything France has to offer to a student, scholarships and job opportunities, to looking at things from a financial lens, Vikash’s helpful tips really engaged the audience! After such an insightful and interactive session, we’re sure almost all are motivated enough to pack their bags and fly off to France!
